PairUp finds people whose profiles could work alongside yours. It does this in two steps.

1. Who shows up on your list

Two sets of rules decide who you see:

  • Their rules about who can find them. Each person sets “Who can find me” on their own profile. If they’ve said “only people on my grade can see me” and you’re a different grade, they won’t appear on your list.
  • Your rules about what you want. In “How I want to find matches”, anything you mark Definite works as a must-have. By default, Grade and Directorate are Definite, so you’ll only see people on your grade who share a directorate with you.

If your list is too short, change “Definite” to “Preferred” in How I want to find matches. That keeps it as a nice-to-have rather than a must-have, and you’ll see more people.

2. The % score on each card

Everyone who shows up gets a score from 0–100 %. The higher the score, the better the fit. Points come from:

  • How well your working days fit together — up to 40 pts. The best fit is when one of you works a day the other has off, so you cover the whole week between you. Two people working the same day at the same time scores zero.
  • Extra shared directorates — up to 20 pts, for each one shared beyond the first.
  • How recently they’ve used PairUp — up to 20 pts. Active in the last two weeks scores full marks; nothing if they haven’t been on in over six months.
  • Same location — 10 pts.
  • Bonuses for “Preferred” matches — up to 30 pts, from anything you’ve marked as Preferred.
  • They’re open to changing their pattern — up to 3 pts.

65–100 % strong fit · 40–64 % partial fit · < 40 % weak fit

Guidance & tips

Making job share work well starts long before your first shared day. These resources walk you through finding the right partner, the conversations to have early, and the practicalities of splitting a role.

Before you match
  • What makes a good job share partner? — traits to look for beyond working days.
  • Working styles and how to describe yours — clean handover vs. close collaboration, and what fits different roles.
  • FCDO flexible working policy (intranet) — entitlements, grading, and what your line manager needs to know.
Things to discuss before you commit
  • Handover practices — how you’ll pass work between you on overlap days.
  • Decision-making and disagreement — who makes calls when you’re not both in the room.
  • Leave, cover, and absence — planning for holidays, sickness, and peak pressures.
  • Meetings and representation — how you’ll divide stakeholder relationships.
  • Performance reviews and objectives — shared vs. individual objectives.
Privacy and what data becomes visible

Anything you enter in the Required info and Optional info sections on your profile becomes visible to anyone who PairUp shows your profile to. That group is governed by your “Who can find me” settings on the profile page.

  • Required info (name, grade, directorates, days, location) is always shown on your match cards.
  • Optional info is shown on your full profile view — matches click “Full profile…” to see it.
  • Who can find me controls apply to everyone. How I want to find matches only affects what you see.

If you’re unsure about something sensitive (e.g. overseas posting plans, caring responsibilities), you can always come back and edit your profile later.

What happens when you delete your profile?
It is a complete, permanent removal.
Your data

Every field you entered — name, grade, directorates, days, location, and all of the optional fields (availability, skills, FTE, working pattern notes, other information) — is permanently removed from PairUp.

What other people see

Your profile disappears from everyone else’s match lists. Anyone who had your profile saved or visible on their screen will no longer see you. There is no archive or recoverable copy.

What is kept

Nothing about you. Your dismissed-profile list, search preferences and consent flag on this browser are also cleared.

Coming back

You can register again at any time by filling in a new profile — it just starts fresh.

Ask yourself
  • Would I be comfortable if a colleague outside my team read this?
  • Is there anything here (e.g. caring responsibilities, health, overseas plans) that’s more than I want to share to get a match?
  • Am I giving enough for someone to decide whether to reach out?
Good to include
  • What you’re currently looking for (a role, a team, a type of work).
  • Your working pattern — the shape of your week, not necessarily the reasons.
  • Skills, experience or specialisms that help a partner size up fit.
  • How you like to work (handover vs. collaboration, meetings, decision-making).
Think twice before including
  • Sensitive personal circumstances — share once you’re talking to a potential partner, not in your public profile.
  • Information about other colleagues or teams.
  • Anything confidential or not already internally known.

Privacy and data policy
FCDO PairUp — internal staff tool
How your data is stored

All data you enter into PairUp is stored exclusively on FCDO internal systems hosted within the UK on Microsoft Azure infrastructure. No data is transferred to, stored on, or processed by any external third-party service or platform.

What this tool is for

PairUp is solely for helping FCDO staff find compatible job share partners. Your profile data is used only for this matching purpose. It will not be used for any other purpose.

Who can see your data

Your profile is visible only to other staff who are also registered on PairUp, and only within the app. Your “Who can find me” settings control which subset of users can see you. No information is shared with HR, line managers, or any other part of the organisation. When you email a potential match, that email is sent from your own mailbox — PairUp is not involved in the conversation.

Your rights and controls

You can update or delete your profile at any time using the “Delete my profile” option. Deletion permanently removes all your data from the system. You are under no obligation to participate, and opting out has no bearing on your employment, performance, or any HR process.

GDPR compliance

PairUp is operated under the lawful basis of legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f) UK GDPR) — specifically, supporting staff mobility and flexible working. The data collected is limited to what is strictly necessary for matching (data minimisation), is not retained beyond active participation, and is held securely. A Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) has been completed for this tool. If you have questions about how your data is handled, contact the FCDO Data Protection Officer.

Ethical commitments

Participation in PairUp is entirely voluntary. The matching algorithm considers only work-related preferences you explicitly provide; it does not infer or use any protected characteristics. No automated decisions are made about your employment — the tool only suggests potential partners for you to consider. All matching is advisory and the final decision rests entirely with you.

Questions? Contact flexible.working@fcdo.gov.uk or the FCDO Data Protection Officer.
About FCDO PairUp
How the app works and how matches are scored
What is PairUp?

PairUp helps FCDO staff find compatible job share partners during the restructure. Finding a partner who wants the same role, works complementary days, and has a compatible working style has historically taken months of informal networking. PairUp makes that search systematic, private, and quick.

You fill in a profile, PairUp shows you a list of potentially compatible colleagues, and you decide who to reach out to. Contact happens by email, directly between the two of you — PairUp does not send messages on anyone’s behalf or store conversations.

How matching works

PairUp first applies gates — a profile only shows up if:

  • Your profile passes their “Who can find me” rules
  • Their profile passes your “How I want to find matches” settings (Grade and Directorate are “Definite” by default)

Profiles that pass are then ranked 0–100 %:

  • Day pattern (up to 40 pts) — “full + non” complements score highest, “full + full” scores zero
  • Extra directorate overlap (up to 20 pts)
  • Recency (up to 20 pts) — active recently scores higher
  • Same location (10 pts)
  • Preferred bonuses (up to 30 pts) — from your search preferences
  • Negotiable pattern (up to 3 pts)

65 %+ is a strong fit, 40–64 % a partial fit, under 40 % a weak fit. Click any match percentage to see the exact breakdown.

What happens when you email a match

The “Email” button opens your email client with the subject and a short message pre-filled, including a summary of your own profile so the other person has the context straight away. You can edit the message before sending. PairUp does not see or store the email.

Tips for getting good matches

Pick a realistic mix of directorates you’d genuinely consider. Be honest about the working days you want. If your default results feel too narrow, relax Grade or Directorates from “Definite” to “Preferred” in search settings.
